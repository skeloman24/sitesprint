Fair questions

Things you’re probably wondering.

Why so cheap?+

Because a one-page site doesn’t cost $3,000 to build. There’s no office, no account manager, and no meetings padded into the price — it’s just me and the work. One page, built well, priced at what it takes.

I already have a Facebook page.+

Keep it — they do different jobs. When someone searches your business name, your own website hands them your hours, menu, and directions in one tap, with no login and no scrolling. And it’s yours: your name, your domain, nobody’s algorithm.

Do we need to meet?+

No. Everything happens by email or text — send your business name and details whenever you have a few minutes, and I take it from there. You’ll hear from me when the site is ready to look at.

Who owns the domain?+

You do, registered in your name. It runs about $12 a year and I pass it through at cost, no markup. Whatever happens down the road, the domain goes with you.

What if I need changes later?+

One round of revisions is included with the build, so you can fine-tune before you approve. After launch, the optional Care Plan ($30/mo) covers content edits within 48 hours, hosting, and a monthly backup — handy when hours, prices, or specials change.

What about photos?+

Use yours — phone photos of your actual shop, food, or chairs beat stock photos every time. Don’t have them handy? I’ll build with clean placeholders and swap in your photos when you send them.
