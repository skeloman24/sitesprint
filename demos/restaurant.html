La nostra cucina

A small room, a long table, one good moon

Luna’s is twelve tables and a bar, and that’s on purpose. The pasta is rolled by hand every afternoon, the ragù goes on before lunch, and the bread comes out when you sit down — not before, not after.

Nonna Luna cooked by one rule: feed people like they’re staying the night. We keep the wine list short, the portions honest, and the lights low enough that dinner takes as long as it wants to.
